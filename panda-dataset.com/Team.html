Professors in Our Team

Lu Fang, Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University

   

Dr. Lu Fang is currently an Associate Professor in Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University. She received Ph.D from Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2011, and B.E. from University of Science and Technology of China in 2007, respectively. Dr. Fang's research interests include computational photography and 3D vision. Dr. Fang used to receive NSFC Excellent Young Scholar Award, Best Student Paper Award in ICME 2017, Finalist of World’s First 10K Best Paper Award in ICME 2017, Finalist of Best Paper Award in ICME 2011 etc. Dr. Fang is currently IEEE Senior Member.
Personal Homepage

Guiguang Ding, Department of Software, Tsinghua University

   

Ding Guiguang is an associate professor in Department of Software, Tsinghua University. He received his Ph.D. from Xidian University in 2004. He is mainly engaged in research work related to multimedia information retrieval and management, including multimedia content understanding, mass image and video retrieval, social media data retrieval and mining, etc. In 2010, he won the second prize of National Science and Technology Progress Award. In 2007, he won the second prize of Science and Technology of Guangdong Province. On August 2, 2019, he was selected into the list of applicants recommended by the National Science Fund for Distinguished Young Scholars in 2019.
Personal Homepage

Qionghai Dai, Department of Automation, Tsinghua University

   

Dr. Qionghai Dai is currently a professor in the Department of Automation, Tsinghua University, and an adjunct professor in the School of Life Science, Tsinghua University. Dr. Dai is also an acadiician of Chinese Acadiy of Engineering. Dr. Dai is expertised in computational photography and stereoscopic vision, and has made pioneering and foundational progress in stereoscopic video and streaming media technologies, and their engineering applications. He received China National Funds for Distinguished Young Scientists in 2005, and was awarded Yangtze river scholars Distinguished Professor in 2009. He was the chief scientist of a "973" project in 2010, and selected in National Hundred, Thousand and Ten Thousand Talent Project in 2014. He was awarded first prize of National Technology Invention Award (rank first) in 2012, and second prize of National Technology Invention Award (rank first) in 2008.
Personal Homepage

David J. Brady, J. W. and H. M. Goodman Endowed Chair in Optical Sciences, University of Arizona
Visiting Professor, Tsinghua-Berkeley Shenzhen Institute, Tsinghua University

   

Dr. David J. Brady is the J. W. and H. M. Goodman Endowed Chair in Optical Sciences at University of Arizona. Brady's research focuses on computational optical imaging. DISP built the world’s first terrestrial gigapixel camera (this work was systiatically introduced in the article titled with "Multiscale gigapixel photography", Nature journal 2012), through the Camputer Laboratory Dr. Brady is leading development of increasingly compact, power efficient and intelligent gigapixel cameras. Brady is also Chief Scientist of Aqueti, which manufactures gigapixel cameras. He is a Fellow of IEEE, SPIE and OSA. He won SPIE Dennis Gabor Award for the development of compressive holographic and tomographic imaging systis in 2013.
